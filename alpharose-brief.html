The Problem

The children living with HNRNPH2-RD and thousands of similar rare neurodevelopmental conditions face a healthcare system that has no approved treatment for them — not because the science is impossible, but because traditional pharma economics make ultra-rare programs commercially unattractive. AlphaRose Therapeutics was built to close that gap: the company integrates proprietary gene therapy (MOE + abcDNA™), AI-driven target discovery via RINAE.AI, and a non-dilutive R&D flywheel through RareLabs to develop precision therapies at a fraction of the traditional cost and timeline.

AlphaRose has raised $4.6M to date, completed 8+ proof-of-concept studies in two years, and identified 300+ targets across its RINAE.AI platform. RareLabs — acquired Q4 2025 — generates $2M+ annually across 16+ fee-for-service customers, funding discovery while building a proprietary patient data asset. IND-enabling work is underway for lead program Rosiphersen, independently validated by NCATS, with Phase 1 targeted by end of 2026.
