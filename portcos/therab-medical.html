Suncoast Fund I · First Exit
Acquired by Natus Medical
TheraB Medical became Suncoast's first fund exit when Natus Medical — a market-leading neonatal care company — acquired the company. Exit proceeds were recycled into Ziora Bio in the same quarter, demonstrating Suncoast's active portfolio management approach and validating the Fund I investment thesis.
The Technology

TheraB Medical developed a wearable phototherapy device that enables treatment of neonatal jaundice at home rather than under hospital bili-lights. Jaundice affects approximately 60% of full-term newborns and up to 80% of preterm infants — making it one of the most common neonatal conditions requiring intervention. The home treatment model cuts length of stay, reduces cost, and dramatically improves family experience by getting infants home faster without sacrificing clinical outcomes.

The commercial thesis was straightforward: hospital-based phototherapy is logistically expensive and clinically unnecessary for the majority of cases. Payer incentives increasingly push toward site-of-care shift. Natus Medical acquired TheraB to integrate this capability into its neonatal portfolio — validating both the technology and the market thesis Suncoast underwrote at entry.
