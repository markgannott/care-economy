About

Raydiant Oximetry is developing Lumerah, a low-cost non-invasive sensor that continuously monitors fetal oxygenation during labor. Current monitoring's 99.8% false positive rate drives 1.2M+ unnecessary C-sections annually in the US — a $15B surgical cost burden with real maternal morbidity. Raydiant has received FDA Breakthrough Device Designation and IDE approval for its Early Feasibility Study. The company is also developing Daisy, a post-partum hemorrhage prevention device. Funded by VCapital, Avestria, CBIV, the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation ($1M grant), NIH, NSF, and Ireland's DTIF.
