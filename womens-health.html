Suncoast Ventures · Fund II Women's Health Thesis

The Women's Health Economy.

The largest underserved market in US healthcare. Still priced like a niche.

Executive Summary

Three gaps. One thesis.

The women's health market is underserved not because demand is small — it's 54 million patients with $26.6B in documented employer burden — but because capital, research, and infrastructure have systematically underinvested in it. The science on menopause and cognitive health is arriving all at once. The digital health correction has left real clinical infrastructure at scrap prices. Employers are adopting meno benefits at 4× the rate of two years ago.

Biology: the menopause → dementia corridor
Part II
The Cognitive Corridor
New Science

Menopause is a neurological inflection point.

The emerging science has reframed the clinical conversation entirely. Menopause is not only an endocrine event — it is a neurological transition with documented downstream dementia risk. Early menopause carries a 47% increased risk (HR 1.47). The research is new enough that no platform has been built around it. The window for a first mover is open now.

The White Space

Nobody owns the menopause → cognitive health corridor. Midi Health owns general menopause telehealth. Maven owns fertility and maternity. The specific nexus — early intervention in menopause to prevent downstream dementia — has no category incumbent. The science is less than two years old. The payer cost base is $781B and growing.

Dementia Economics
Payer Imperative

$781 billion. Payers have to move.

The US dementia cost base has crossed $781B annually — larger than the entire Medicare prescription drug spend. At this scale, dementia prevention is no longer a research priority; it is a payer imperative. The menopause → dementia corridor is the most plausible upstream intervention point, and the research supporting it arrived in 2024–2025.

Capital: the correction creates the buying window
Part III
The Correction
Market Signal

$29.1B → $10.1B. Real infrastructure at trough prices.

The 2020–2022 digital health boom deployed $58.5B across ~1,741 deals. Much of that capital went into women's health platforms at valuations that assumed a DTC consumer market that didn't materialize. The correction has been severe and indiscriminate — creating a multi-year buying window for acquirers who can add payer contracting, employer channel, and clinical credibility to distressed-but-viable platforms.

Market Signal
Employer Channel

4% to 18%. Employers are ahead of the market.

The fastest-moving buyer in women's health is not the health plan — it is the employer. Menopause benefit adoption has accelerated from 4% to 18% of large employers in two years. At 18%, the category has cleared the proof-of-concept bar. The remaining 82% of large employers represent an addressable sales motion for any platform that can show the burden data.

Employer Channel

4%→18%

Menopause benefit adoption at large US employers in two years — the fastest category movement in the women's health benefits market.

Payer Movement

$781B

The dementia cost base makes preventive menopause care a payer imperative. CMS and commercial payers are both moving toward coverage. The math forces it.

Exit Landscape

6–8×

Healthcare IT M&A revenue multiples for platforms with contracted payer or employer revenue — vs ~4.8× for generic B2B SaaS. Capstone Partners 2024.

What gets built across the full arc of women's health
Part IV
Venture Categories
Portfolio Thesis

Four categories. One arc.

The Suncoast women's health portfolio is organized around the full clinical arc: from reproductive care and maternal monitoring through the menopause transition and into the cognitive health corridor. Each category has a specific commercial mechanism — employer channel, payer contracting, clinical evidence, or platform infrastructure.

Menopause & Functional Medicine
The Clinical Core

DTC-to-enterprise conversion: functional medicine and menopause telehealth platforms with real patients and real retention — restructured to contract with employers and payers. The gap is not product; it is channel. GritWell is the anchor.

Maternal Health & Monitoring
The Device Layer

Remote monitoring and diagnostic devices for maternal and fetal health — with CMS reimbursement pathways and health system partnerships. Clinical evidence is built in. Commercial runway is payer and hospital contracting. Bloomlife · Raydiant.

Reproductive Health & Fertility
The Innovation Layer

MedTech and biotech tackling reproductive health — from IVF culture media to contraceptive innovation. IP-protected, regulatory-pathwayed, exit-ready to medtech strategics. Ziora Bio · Next Life Sciences.

Care Coordination & Family Tech
The Platform Layer

Infrastructure connecting women to the care they manage — for themselves and their families. NICU transitions, family operating systems, and care coordination that turns fragmented healthcare into a manageable experience. Nicolette · Trellis · Ohai.
