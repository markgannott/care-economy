The children living with HNRNPH2-RD and thousands of similar rare neurodevelopmental conditions face a healthcare system that has no approved treatment for them — not because the science is impossible, but because traditional pharma economics make ultra-rare programs commercially unattractive. AlphaRose Therapeutics was built to close that gap: the company integrates proprietary gene therapy (MOE + abcDNA™), AI-driven target discovery via RINAE.AI, and a non-dilutive R&D flywheel through RareLabs to develop precision therapies at a fraction of the traditional cost and timeline. Lead program Rosiphersen has demonstrated 100% survival rescue in murine models of HNRNPH2-RD and been independently validated by NCATS in human iPSC-derived neurons.

AlphaRose has raised $4.6M to date, completed 8+ proof-of-concept studies in two years, and identified 300+ targets across its RINAE.AI platform. RareLabs — acquired Q4 2025 — generates $2M+ annually across 16+ fee-for-service customers, funding discovery while building a proprietary patient data asset. IND-enabling work is underway for Rosiphersen with Phase 1 targeted by end of 2026. The company is backed by Termeer Family Office, Jackson Lab, and independently validated by NCATS/NIH, giving it a non-dilutive credibility stack rare at the Seed stage.

People
Patient Population
30M Americans live with rare disease. 95% have no approved treatment. HNRNPH2-RD causes intellectual disability, seizures, and absent speech with no clinical options. AlphaRose's RINAE.AI platform covers 300+ identified targets across this chronically underserved population.
Plans

Not the primary channel at this stage. Rare disease reimbursement routes through specialty pharmacy, orphan drug designation, and patient assistance — not commercial payer networks. Plans become relevant post-IND.
