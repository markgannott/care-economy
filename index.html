Bigger than Medicaid. Becoming investable now.

63.0M Americans provide unpaid care each year. They contribute 53.0B hours of labor annually, equal to 25.5M full-time workers. Executive Summary

The Ratio is the Thesis

  • At 53.0B hours annually — equivalent to 6.05M person-years — unpaid caregiving is the largest unpriced labor system in the country.
  • Traditional replacement-cost models price caregiving at a worker's wage. Opportunity-cost methodology measures what caregivers actually forgo: income, labor participation, retirement accumulation, and long-term economic capacity.
  • That shift moves the market from a $642B estimate to $1.6T.
  • The ratio is the thesis: enormous economic value, limited dedicated infrastructure, and a market now moving toward coordination, reimbursement, employer support, and AI-enabled continuity.
