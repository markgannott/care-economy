The Research

Three papers. One economic argument.

The care economy thesis is built on three sequential white papers, each extending the prior. Together they establish the valuation, the compounding structure of the burden, and the measurable pathway to economic recovery.

Paper I

Repricing the Caregiver Economy

$1.6T

The US care economy has been systematically undervalued by every prior methodology. Displaced labor valuation — pricing caregiving at actual opportunity cost, not replacement cost — produces $1.62 trillion. That changes the TAM calculation, the company multiples, and the LP conviction.

Paper II

The Coordination Economy

354 hrs/yr

The $1.6T understates the true cost. Primary caregivers carry 354 additional unpaid hours per year, but the damage compounds — nominal leisure is cognitively occupied, sleep is fragmented, administrative burden is constant. A fragmented hour has lower economic value than an uninterrupted one.

Paper III

Recoverable Time & Economic Capacity

$98.6B/hr

Every hour of coordination burden reduced across 63 million caregivers = $98.6 billion in recovered national economic value. Restoring continuity doesn’t just return time — it amplifies the yield of all remaining hours. The platform delivering this is building compounding infrastructure, not a time-tracking app.

Part I
Scale — the $1.6 trillion hiding in plain sight
Valuation Methodology
Paper I

Why the number is $1.6 trillion — not less.

Three methodologies. Three very different answers. Only one that belongs on an investment thesis.

Minimum Wage Floor

$384B

Question asked: What if we paid caregivers federal minimum wage?

53B hours × $7.25/hr. Values the work at the floor of the US labor market. Lower bound only — not a valuation.

Shadow Wage / Replacement Cost

~$640B

Question asked: What would it cost to hire a professional caregiver?

53B hours × home health aide rate (~$15–17/hr, BLS 2023). The flaw: prices the work at the replacement worker’s wage, not the caregiver’s opportunity cost.

Displaced Labor — Gannott 2026

$1.62T

Question asked: What income did caregivers actually forgo?

53B hours × sex-adjusted opportunity-cost wages ($27.96/hr women, $34.05/hr men), BLS ATUS + ACS 2022. The displaced labor methodology — same framework national accounts use to price informal production.
